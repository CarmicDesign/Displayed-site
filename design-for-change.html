Project Brief

As designers you have the opportunity to use your creativity for good. From reducing ocean waste and environmental degradation to human rights for refugees, everyone has something they believe in and stand for.

What do you support or believe needs to change in the world? Assuming as a graphic designer, working in a studio environment, managing a social change campaign for a NFP organisation. You will manage the project from research, initial concept development to marketing strategy, design, implementation and monitoring. Design digital advertising output to the platforms best suited to your campaign and target audience.
‍
There are 3 parts to this project:

PART A:

Concept presentation at design meeting

  • Cause/social change
  • Argument for and against
  • Overview of client, industry, services, products/campaigns
  • Target audience – characteristics and digital markets/arenas to access them Concepts for strategy and campaign
  • What is your aim? What do you want your audience to do? What type of campaign is it?
  • Explain key ethical principles relevant to advertising industry that impact

PART B:

Design and produce 3 advertisements for your social change campaign for 3 separate digital formats

  1. Digital publication: single or double page spread for chosen digital magazine/journal
  2. Social media platform: appropriate platform for the chosen cause/issue
  3. Your choice of identified format

PART C:

Include ePortfolio project report

Research

Our modern life has become fine tuned towards worldwide ethical and environmental issues throwing away cultures, customs and close-minded perceptions.

With growing concern over the climate change, uncontrollable pandemic, humanitarian tragedies and ecological emergencies, it’s very disheartening that it’s taken us this long to envisage about being more environment sensible.

This campaign is intended to create awareness to the eCommerce retailers with the aim to reduce carbon footprint with a switch from existing general packaging to eco-friendly tape packaging that are beneficial in saving our planet thereby saving ourselves.

Platform Chosen

Print Platform

Our chosen platform for the Magazine ad (2-page spread) is Retail World Magazine. Retail World magazine is a content rich publication covering the reflections from retailers, retail news headlines, product launch highlights, industry insights (retail performance, trends and grocery outlook).

As apparent from its’ name itself, this magazine tends to match our preferences and likely to reach most of the business, entrepreneurs and merchants more easily with both its print and online publication with their own dedicated website.

Nevertheless, Retail World has no bias and supports a wide range of voices from the broader retail industry.

Digital Platform

To maximise our reach to targeted E-Commerce merchants’ mainstream, we preferred to use Instagram and LinkedIn as digital platforms because social media like Instagram is powerful tool that allows users to upload media by organising through hashtags and geographical tagging. Nevertheless, our post can be affiliated with influencers which will help to heighten its intended purpose and reach.

LinkedIn, on the other hand is specific to professional networking which room to almost all the entrepreneurs, stakeholder, corporate entity, NGOs, Government and policy makers making it the right platform to reach the intended audience.

Our visually appealing image with strong environment copywriting post will aim to create awareness to our target audience convincing them to switch to more sustainable eco-tape packaging alternatives.
