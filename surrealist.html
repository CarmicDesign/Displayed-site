Project Brief

PART 1Create an A5 (148 mm x 210mm) surrealist self-portrait image using Photoshop. The image must include a 3mm bleed, use CMYK colour mode, include trim marks and will be submitted in a range of image formats.
‍
PART 2Produce and present a series of thumbnail sketches of possible concepts for your surrealist self-portrait.
‍
PART 3Upload the 2 most creative concepts to an eLearn forum (The Melting Pot) for review and feedback. This post is to provide links or image examples of two works/artists from which you are drawing inspiration and ideas.
‍
PART 4Deliver a presentation document of the final surrealist self-portrait. This document is to include: An overview of your creative vision – what are you communicating about yourself through the surrealist form? A step by step creative process pathway including the initial concept sketches, images used for inspiration, first digital draft, list of suggestions from peers culminating in the final refined version. Offer an insight into the technique(s) used to arrive at final composition and how this treatment has fulfilled your creative vision.

The self-portrait should communicate something about yourself as a person so carefully consider the elements you will incorporate into this image. You will need to start off with a base photo of yourself with adequate resolution for the project and then incorporate other graphic components into the image, experimenting with manipulations and treatments in Photoshop. The final image must be built using layers and combine various additional images that convey something about your personality and ultimately produce a captivating and thought provoking image. Try to keep all the ingredients in your portrait original to avoid copyright issues and be as creative and experimental as possible.

Creative Vision

How can earth be such a beautiful place to live in without nature and its existence. Over the last few decade human dominance over nature has led to warnings of climate change, extinction of animals, biodiversity loss, and depletions in natural resources. It is never too late to act before human power is overwhelmed by the power of nature.

My idea for this portrait was to presume the feelings of tree where they can just see around and couldn’t speak or move. Sometimes I just think to myself that what if we are in their place, would it be the same situation?

Besides, I always wanted to manipulate my portrait with mother nature and this was the right opportunity to spill out my creative juices.

Concept Sketches

Concept One
For this concept, I thought of trying to play around with my portrait with mixture of sketch effect. How I would like to try shades from the sketches, if only we can draw and use. Other thing is, experimenting myself with different hair style and beards. It was simply an Adobe Photoshop experiment.

Concept Two
This was actually my consolidated and surpassed concept I must say. Sometimes walking in woods wonders me what if the trees were us and will the earth be different. Manipulating portrait with nature has always been one of my curiosity and this was the perfect hands on.

First Draft & Peer Support

Peers suggestions were:

  • Green leafy eyebrows would be good
  • Consistency of colour and more green leaves on forehead for smoother transition from tree to face
  • Add vignette effect and add shadow beneath butterfly to give it some depth
  • Be interesting to have a caterpillar crawling near trunk or a cocoon to create a meaningful connection to the butterfly
  • Skin tones are a little dark

Final Design

The tools and techniques that I used for this design was sufficient enough to cater for what I wanted to convey. Adobe Photoshop is one of the best tool to play with raster images.
‍
The pictures I used here is my own Portrait and few from unsplash.com which has got tones of beautiful royalty free images. Nevertheless, I managed to collect tree pictures with amazing trunk texture and elegant roots which really helped me to pop up the design.
The overall looks of the portrait with vignette definitely added upon cinematic effect. The depth of picture is also achieved by slight blurred background. The choice of colour perfectly blends with the subject with warmth feel portraying the dry, wonder and deserted.
‍
Lastly, the blend from trunk to hand and then to leaves did a good job characterising surrealism. Butterflies and caterpillar added the feel of nature.
